## Article 31: The Right to Play

The UN Convention on the Rights of the Child enshrines play as a fundamental right under Article 31. This isn't just about recreation‚Äîit's about recognizing that play is essential for:
- Healthy development
- Learning and education
- Social inclusion
- Conflict prevention and peacebuilding
- Mental health and well-being

For children facing medical challenges, this right becomes even more critical. When illness threatens to steal childhood joy, therapeutic play becomes a lifeline to normalcy, hope, and healing.

### For Healthcare Providers:
